What is factor analysis?

All business processes are interconnected and can change under the influence of various factors, to identify and examine the extent of the impact on the business which helps factor analysis.

Factor analysis is a comprehensive and systematic study of the impact of individual factors on the company's bottom line.

The main goal of business analysis is to find the most effective ways to increase the profitability of the firm.

Factor analysis of the business assesses the efficiency of the company and reveals possible ways to increase the company's profit. Factor analysis has practical usage, so it should be carried out on a regular basis, it reveals the necessary data to make effective business decisions and contributes to harmonious regulation of cash flows within the company.

What is the tasks of factor analysis of business processes?

  • Selecting factors for the research;
  • Classification of indicators to ensure a systemic approach;
  • Establishing relationships between performance and factor performance;
  • Determining the influence of factors and assessing them.

The main stages of factor analysis:

  • Factor selection;
  • Grouping factors;
  • Modeling the link between performance and factor indicators;
  • Assessing the impact and role of factors on the value of the performance;
  • Introduction of the factor model

Thus, factor analysis of the business helps to establish the volume of sales of products, the sale cost and price of goods that will increase the company's profits, as well as to identify the product that is sold best, and the product that is the least in demand, to reduce the cost of its production in the future. This method of economic analysis of the company's activity plays a crucial role in the restructuring of the business, as well as for recently opened companies.

Factor analysis of the business is a complex system of calculations and analysis of indicators, requiring special theoretical knowledge and analytical skills.
