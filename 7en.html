Why create a franchise?

Any successful business at some stage reaches the point of growth. And then there are two main development options:

  • Expanding the business by opening its own branches;
  • Scale by franchise.

The second option - a business franchise - is more attractive for most entrepreneurs by receiving a monthly fee for the right to use the brand, as well as low costs for opening branches themselves.

The main benefits of creating a business franchise:

  • Creating new sources of income and marketing channels;
  • Minimum costs of setting up units and managing the network;
  • The distribution of the product to a large area of the market;
  • Transfer of commercial risks to franchisees.

To become a franchise, the company must meet the following conditions:

  • Be profitable.

    • If the company itself does not make a profit, then the franchisees will not be able to earn anything.

  • Have long-term prospects.

    • It is necessary to take into account the terms of payback of the business for franchisees, so that he had the opportunity to earn on the brand image. If there are only short-term prospects for the company (for example, up to 2 years), the offer to sell the franchise will not be relevant.

  • Provide assistance at the start for franchisees.

    • At the start-up stage, franchisees need the support of a franchisor company. With it, they will be able to become profitable and successful in a shorter time.

What questions arise at the stage of creating the franchise?

Transforming a successful business into a franchise and starting to attract branches is not easy, it requires a number of operations that must be properly conducted.

  • Industry analysis;
  • Creating and registering a brand ;
  • Making a contract;
  • Admission fee (share fee);
  • The size of the royalties;
  • Formulating commitments and responsibilities;
  • Making a provision on safeguards;
  • Transfer of know-how and operational manual;
  • Creating a business plan;
  • Profiling the franchise;
  • Developing or setting up a "leading point";
  • Writing a marketing plan and managing a plan for potential affiliates;
  • Writing information materials, dossiers and brochures.
