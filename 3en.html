What is a business plan?

A business plan is a detailed description of the future project, its activities with calculations and the prospect of development for the next couple of years.

The purpose of developing a business plan?

The business plan answers the following questions:

  • How much does it take to start a project?
  • In what time will the project pay off?

Business plan, first of all, is a necessity for potential investors or the bank in which you are going to apply for a loan, as its main task is to convince potential investors and partners that your project really deserves their attention, it is interesting from the point of investments and will recoup the funds invested in it. Creating a business plan of the enterprise is also necessary for the company, as the development of the company's business plan pursues not only external goals (getting a loan, attracting investments), but also internal.

The internal goals of developing a business plan include:

  • Strategic goals, for example, achieving a leading position in the market, entering the international market, updating the production base, developing personnel, etc.
  • Finding and justifying the arguments in favor of the feasibility of reorganizing an existing enterprise or creating a new one.
  • Determining for the staff of the enterprise the tasks expressed in qualitative and quantitative indicators.
  • Analysis of possible markets, their assessment.
  • Calculating the potential profitability of products or services provided.
  • Analysis and evaluation of the financial and material situation of the company.

Therefore, a professionally drawn up business plan not only reveals the main aspects of the company's activities, but also analyzes the possible risks that the company may face, gives recommendations for their reduction.

What is included in the business plan?

The business plan includes a product or service description, market analysis, production plan, enterprise organizational structure, product promotion marketing strategy, and a financial plan with all the basic calculations. Therefore, in most cases, business plans are strictly confidential documents and the subject of the company's trade secrets.

The procedure for developing a business plan:

Business planning consists of several stages:

  • Preparations. At this stage, it is necessary to determine the purpose of drawing up a business plan, sources of information and stakeholders, as well as to develop a complete structure of the document.
  • Gathering and analysing the information required to write a business plan, on the basis of which the main areas of the company's activities will be highlighted in the future, the weaknesses and strengths of the business will be determined and the opportunities and risks are assessed.
  • Calculating the project's sustainability indicators to external indicators: liquidity, business activity, profitability, return on investment, etc., based on the data obtained, the conclusion is made of the competitiveness of the project.
  • Discussing the plan with employees of all parts of the organization, making adjustments if necessary.
  • The final stage is the final version, approval and design of the plan.
